Why it's Important to Be Prepared for Layoffs

16 Mar 2023By Alejandro

Yesterday, I had a call with Martin, a full-stack software engineer whom I had known for some time in San Francisco. He told me about his situation where he had been laid off and had no more than 30 days to find another job.

The pressure he was feeling was self-imposed. There was no need to rush into job applications without a plan. In fact, my recommendation for him was to go to France, prepare for interviews, and make a five-year plan.

As I asked him more questions, I discovered many reasons why he needed help from a mentor and the "I can do it all" engineer mentality. However, that attitude had not worked for him in the last five years, and he could have easily had a minimum of 1 million dollars if he had made better choices. After all, in life, bad choices give bad consequences.

He had also not applied for a green card, which meant he couldn't have better options. For example, he couldn't apply for more than one job. He had also not made good decisions about the companies he worked for. Many public companies provide a 4x increase in 4-5 years, and taking startup jobs is riskier.

He had given up on his dreams, such as owning a house in Paris, spending time with his family, returning to Europe, and pursuing his hobbies, which were far from the options he had found.

He even asked me why we hadn't worked together five years ago.

It's easy to make decisions in hindsight, but a mentor can help you be in a better position, and what may seem like an expense is only an investment.

If you don't want to end up like Martin and want to take action right now, book 30 minutes to help you take control of your life.

Stay strong,
